Making coffee with your AeroPress

An AeroPress is a great way to make a single cup of coffee at a time. Once you have a kettle of hot water, it only takes a few moments to make coffee with the AeroPress, so it's also great for making two or three cups in quick succession. It doesn't use any electricity, so it's great for camping or trips.

Collect up your supplies. Here we have all the things you'll need. A kettle of water, your AeroPress (all three pieces), a filter, a scale, ground coffee, and a stirring tool. In this case, I've already weighed and ground the coffee. You can start by putting your kettle on to boil, and then get everything ready while it is heating up.

Grind some coffee (17 grams) to a fine grind (as you would use for a cone filter automatic drip maker). note: this recipe does not follow the general 1:17 rule; it makes a concentrated extract similar to espresso.

Place a paper filter into the filter basket.

Turn your AeroPress upside down. Slide the plunger to the bottom of the 4 circle. This is counter to the directions included with your AeroPress, but placing it upside down prevents it from dripping before you want to push down the plunger.

Pour your grounds into the body of your AeroPress.

Boil some water. Pour a little water into your basket to rinse off the filter and pre warm your mug.
Pour the water out of your mug.

Pour in water up to the top of the 2 circle.

Give it a quick stir, clap the basket on, and invert over the mug.

Press the plunger down. Be gentle but firm. If you press too hard or fast, the filter will clog with fines and it will be nearly impossible to push the rest out. Stop when it starts hissing. At that point, the water is out and you are done.

Pull the basket off and push the plunger all the way though to pop the puck out. Hold it over the compost or trash can, and it should just come out in one piece.

Coffee! Top off your mug with hot water (if desired).
